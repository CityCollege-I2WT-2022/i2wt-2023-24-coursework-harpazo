Onii Collective

History

The nomadic origins of the Onii are etched into the collective memory, and ancient artifacts, carefully preserved in arboreal archives, tell stories of their journey. The Elder Council, seated within a sacred grove, not only guides political decisions but also serves as the living repository of ancestral wisdom. The decision-making process involves a ritualistic dance, where council members weave intricate patterns symbolizing unity and consensus. The council's deliberations take place in a Hall of Echoes, a structure designed to amplify the harmonious discussions. The Onii practice a unique form of oral history, where storytellers weave sagas using luminescent scrolls that illuminate the darkened groves during communal gatherings.

The Eldertrees, towering giants with roots entwined in the ancient soil, serve as both witnesses and chroniclers of the Onii's history. Their bark, etched with ancient carvings, tells tales of triumphs, trials, and the cyclical dance of life. On special occasions, the Onii gather around these Eldertrees, engaging in ceremonies where the elders interpret the carvings, relaying the stories to the younger generation. The Eldertrees are considered the living archives, and it is believed that those who listen to their whispers gain wisdom and a deeper understanding of the Onii's journey.
