Cynocephali Confederacy

A collection of packs of cynocephali, a dog headed race. Each pack has its own autonomy, and the leader of each pack gathers twice a year in a council.

The Cynocephali Confederacy, nestled between the imposing Mov to the East and the encroaching Yek to the West, is a diverse and sprawling expanse that unites the distinctive packs of canine-headed beings. This land, once marked by territorial disputes and inter-pack conflicts, now stands as a testament to the strength of unity forged in response to external threats.

Stretching across varying terrains, from dense forests to expansive plains and mountainous regions, the Confederacy embodies the resilience of the Cynocephali. Each pack maintains its autonomy, governing its territory with a blend of communal cooperation and individual identity.

The heart of the Confederacy is marked by the Council Grounds, where pack leaders convene biannually to discuss matters of collective importance. These gatherings, known as the Councils of Howls, serve as a symbol of solidarity, as leaders communicate through a melodic symphony of vocalizations that transcend linguistic barriers.

The landscape is adorned with Scent-Marking Glyphs, intricate patterns that tell stories of territorial boundaries, historical events, and cultural milestones. These glyphs, painted on natural canvases like trees and rocks, serve as a tangible representation of the interconnectedness of the packs.

In times of celebration or crisis, the Cynocephali come together for vibrant festivals, marked by anthems, chants, and the rhythmic pounding of paws. Festive energy reverberates through the Confederacy, emphasizing the shared heritage and collective spirit that binds the packs.

As guardians of their autonomy, the Cynocephali have developed a keen awareness of the threats surrounding their borders. They stand ready to defend their diverse territories, utilizing the unique skills of each pack, be it navigating dense forests, scaling mountains, or patrolling open plains.
